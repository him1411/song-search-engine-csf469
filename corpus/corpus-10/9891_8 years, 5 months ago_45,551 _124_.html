It describes a programming language called Simula 67. Simula 67 is an extended version of Algol 60, which contains some extra simulation capabilities. It has these things called "classes" which each describe the behavior of a bunch of individual "instances." I encourage those of you who lived through this history to confirm and/or deny any parts of my speculation that you can.I am more familiar with the more recent progression, so I\'ll start there.
